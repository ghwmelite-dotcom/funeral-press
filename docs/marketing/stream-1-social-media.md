## 2. Five Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Educational (Funeral Planning Tips & Checklists)

**Goal:** Position FuneralPress as the trusted authority on funeral planning in Ghana.

**Content types:** Threads, infographics, carousels, polls, checklists
**Frequency:** 2-3x per week
**Tone:** Helpful, clear, practical

**Example topics:**
- Step-by-step funeral planning guide
- What documents you need before a burial in Ghana
- How to write a tribute for a loved one
- Funeral budget breakdown for a typical Ghanaian funeral
- Checklist: 7 days before the funeral
- How to coordinate family members across multiple countries

### Pillar 2: Product Showcases (Features, Templates, Before/After)

**Goal:** Drive sign-ups and usage by showing what FuneralPress can do.

**Content types:** Screen recordings, mockup images, before/after comparisons, feature spotlights
**Frequency:** 2-3x per week
**Tone:** Proud, warm, professional

**Example topics:**
- "Design a funeral brochure in 5 minutes" (screen recording)
- Template showcase: Black & Gold theme vs. Kente Gold theme
- Feature spotlight: AI Tribute Writer
- Before/after: handwritten programme vs. FuneralPress design
- New feature announcement: One-Week Celebration Posters
- "How to publish an online memorial page"

### Pillar 3: Cultural (Ghanaian Funeral Traditions & Adinkra Symbols)

**Goal:** Celebrate and preserve Ghanaian funeral culture while building emotional resonance.

**Content types:** Carousels, single images with captions, threads, short videos
**Frequency:** 1-2x per week
**Tone:** Reverent, educational, celebratory

**Example topics:**
- The meaning behind popular Adinkra symbols used in funerals
- Why Ghanaian funerals are celebrations of life
- The tradition of wearing red and black
- Fantasy coffins: Art and meaning
- The role of the family head in funeral planning
- How different ethnic groups observe one-week celebrations
- The significance of funeral cloth and kente at ceremonies

### Pillar 4: Emotional / Community (Grief Support & Family Stories)

**Goal:** Build trust and community by showing empathy and creating a safe space.

**Content types:** Quote cards, anonymized stories, polls, gentle threads
**Frequency:** 1-2x per week
**Tone:** Gentle, compassionate, human

**Example topics:**
- "What I wish someone told me when I lost my parent"
- Grief is not linear — a thread on processing loss
- Poll: "What helped you most during a difficult loss?"
- Community spotlight: A family who used FuneralPress to honour their grandmother
- "It is okay to not be okay" — grief support resources in Ghana
- "How do you remember your loved ones?" — open question

### Pillar 5: Behind the Scenes (Building FuneralPress, Founder Journey)

**Goal:** Humanize the brand and build connection with the tech community.

**Content types:** Threads, casual photos, screenshots, personal reflections
**Frequency:** 1x per week
**Tone:** Honest, reflective, relatable

**Example topics:**
- Why I built FuneralPress
- The tech stack behind FuneralPress
- A week in the life of building a product for grief
- User feedback that made us cry
- Challenges of building for the African market
- "We just hit 1,000 designs created" — milestone celebrations

### Script 1: "How to Plan a Funeral in Ghana: Step by Step" (X Thread)

**Tweet 1:**
Planning a funeral in Ghana can feel overwhelming, especially when you are grieving.

Here is a clear, step-by-step guide to help you through it.

A thread:

**Tweet 2:**
Step 1: Notify the family head (Abusuapanyin)

Before anything else, the extended family head must be informed. They will help coordinate family decisions — from the burial date to financial contributions.

This is tradition, and it matters.

**Tweet 3:**
Step 2: Obtain the death certificate

Visit the hospital or morgue where the body is held. You will need:
- Deceased's ID (Ghana Card or passport)
- Hospital records
- A family member to sign

The Births & Deaths Registry issues the official certificate.

**Tweet 4:**
Step 3: Choose a burial date

Families typically consult the family head, the church, and the cemetery. In Ghana, funerals are often held on Saturdays. Book early — popular dates fill up fast.

Consider: Will diaspora family need travel time?

Funeral costs in Ghana can range from GHS 15,000 to GHS 150,000+. The biggest costs:
- Morgue fees

## 6. Engagement Strategy

### Comment Response Framework

| Comment Type | Response Approach | Response Time |
|--------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Positive feedback | Thank them warmly, ask to share with others | Within 2 hours |
| Feature request | Acknowledge, note it, share excitement | Within 4 hours |
| Question about features | Answer clearly, link to the tool | Within 2 hours |
| Grief/emotional sharing | Respond with empathy first, never sell | Within 1 hour |
| Negative/critical | Acknowledge, ask for details privately, stay gracious | Within 2 hours |
| Spam/trolling | Ignore or hide. Do not engage. | N/A |

### Response Templates

**For grief sharing:**
> "Thank you for sharing this. We are sorry for your loss, and we are honoured that you trusted us with your family's story. If there is anything we can help with, we are here."

**For positive feedback:**
> "This means so much to us. Thank you. If you know a family who could use FuneralPress, please share it with them. Every share helps a family in need."

**For feature requests:**
> "That is a brilliant idea. We have noted it and will look into it. Thank you for helping us make FuneralPress better for everyone."

### Trending Topics Engagement Rules

1. **Engage when:** The topic relates to Ghanaian culture, funeral traditions, grief, family, community, African tech, or design tools
2. **Avoid when:** The topic is political, controversial, or disrespectful toward death/grief
3. **How to engage:** Add genuine value. Share a relevant fact, resource, or perspective. Never force a product mention into a trending conversation about loss
4. **Newsjacking:** If a public figure passes, do NOT use it to promote FuneralPress. Instead, share a respectful tribute post with no product mention

### X (Twitter) — Pinned Tweet

**What to pin:** The founder story thread (Day 1 content)

**Why:** The founder story thread serves as the single most important first impression for new visitors. It answers three critical questions: What is FuneralPress? Why does it exist? Why should I care?

**Pin 1:** "What is FuneralPress?" carousel (Script 3)

**When to rotate:** Monthly. Replace Pin 2 and Pin 3 with the best-performing posts from the previous month. Keep Pin 1 (product explainer) until a better-performing version is created.

## Appendix: Content Production Notes

### Visual Style Guidelines

- **Colours:** Use brand colours consistently. Dark backgrounds (black, deep burgundy) with gold accents work best for funeral content
- **Typography:** Clean, serif fonts for headlines. Sans-serif for body text. Never use casual or playful fonts
- **Photography:** Respectful, dignified imagery. Flowers, candles, Adinkra symbols, kente patterns. Never use stock photos of people crying
- **Mockups:** Use FuneralPress product mockups as the primary visual. They showcase the product and maintain brand consistency
- **Tone in visuals:** Warm, not cold. Dignified, not somber. Celebratory, not mournful
